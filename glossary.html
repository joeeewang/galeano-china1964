Person · Uruguay

Eduardo Galeano

1940–2015

Journalist, editorial secretary at Marcha. Editor-in-chief at Época (1963). Author of China 1964: Crónica de un Desafío (1964) — his first book, and the first account of the journey. Traveled through China from late September to early November 1963, visiting Beijing, Wuhan, Hangzhou, Shanghai, and Guangzhou. Conducted 24 interviews, including with Premier Zhou Enlai and the former Last Emperor Puyi. Maintained a skeptical, journalistic stance — committed to producing neither "slander nor dithyramb." Chinese officials classified him as a "non-party person" and "cautious and skeptical." China 1964 was never translated or published in mainland China, Hong Kong, or Taiwan.

Person · Uruguay

Guillermo Bernhard

c. 1930s–

Economist and agronomist. Traveled with Galeano throughout China in fall 1963. Made first contact with the Czechoslovak intelligence agency (StB) in January 1963 — the same year as the journey. Became chairman of Época in late 1964 and a formal StB agent in 1965. His travelogue Crónicas Chinas, published in Época between November 8–18, 1963, affirmed Chinese official claims on every point the state was most actively promoting: the stability of people's communes, agricultural recovery, and Soviet inadequacy. Was selected and translated by Reference News editors, placed on the front page on January 3, 1964. Galeano was unaware of Bernhard's intelligence connections.

Person · China

Zhou Enlai 周恩来

1898–1976

Premier of the People's Republic of China (1949–1976). Received Galeano in an exclusive interview on November 4, 1963, at the Beijing Hotel — lasting one hour and thirty minutes. Discussed China's exclusion from the United Nations, revolutionary movements in Asia, Africa, and Latin America, the limits of Soviet-American coexistence, and China's nuclear program. Also present were Zhou Erfu and Ren Yinglun. Galeano had repeatedly requested a meeting with Mao Zedong; the Zhou interview was likely arranged as a compromise. The meeting was recorded on the front page of People's Daily on November 5, 1963.

Organization · Uruguay

Marcha

Est. 1939–1974

Independent left-wing weekly newspaper based in Montevideo, Uruguay. Founded and directed by Carlos Quijano. One of the most influential publications of the Latin American left in the mid-twentieth century. Known for its strong editorial independence: Quijano repeatedly refused to run StB-paid articles, in contrast to Época. Galeano served as editorial secretary and later as a key contributor. The paper was shut down by the Uruguayan military dictatorship in 1974.

Organization · Uruguay

Época

Est. 1962–1967

Left-wing daily newspaper based in Montevideo, Uruguay (1962–1967). Edited by Galeano in 1963; later chaired by Bernhard (from late 1964). Funded by multiple sources: Cuban and Brazilian exile networks, and suspected Chinese economic support. Infiltrated by the Czechoslovak intelligence agency StB, which used the paper to promote pro-communist sentiment and discredit the United States across Latin America. Shut down in December 1967. Its compromised editorial independence stands in direct contrast to the weekly Marcha.

Organization · Uruguay

PSU

Est. 1910–present

Partido Socialista del Uruguay (Uruguayan Socialist Party). Founded in 1910. Galeano, Bernhard, and Carlos Machado were all members. The PSU occupied a distinctive position within the Uruguayan left, separate from both the Moscow-aligned PCU (Communist Party of Uruguay) and the Cuban-oriented Movimiento de Liberación Nacional. Its members navigated the ideological confusion produced by the Sino-Soviet split with varying degrees of alignment.

Organization · Czechoslovakia

StB

Est. 1945–1990

Státní bezpečnost — Czechoslovak State Security (1945–1990). Operated under Soviet oversight within the Eastern Bloc intelligence network. Established its first Montevideo station in 1961. Used local Latin American newspapers, including Época, to discredit the United States and promote pro-communist sentiment. Made first contact with Guillermo Bernhard in January 1963; Bernhard became a formal agent in 1965. Although the StB operated under Soviet oversight, it apparently tolerated Bernhard's pro-China content in Época — likely because its primary interest was anti-American propaganda, not policing the Sino-Soviet dispute. Source: Michal Zourek, JLAS (2024), using declassified StB archives.

Organization · China

Reference News 参考消息

Est. 1931–present

参考消息 (Cānkǎo Xiāoxi). Party-state internal publication providing senior officials and cadres with direct, unedited translations of foreign press reports — offering a rare window into global perspectives. On January 3, 1964, its editors selected and translated Bernhard's travelogue Crónicas Chinas, placing it on the front page alongside a translated New York Times commentary describing 1963 as a year of failures for both the United States and the Soviet Union. This editorial juxtaposition recruited Bernhard's voice into a larger argument about China's rise as the Third World's leading power. Galeano's China 1964, by contrast, received no coverage.
